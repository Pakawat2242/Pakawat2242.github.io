TMMO 5414: Our interested area

Alzheimer's disease is a brain disorder that gets worse over time. It's characterized by changes in the brain that lead to deposits of certain proteins. Alzheimer's disease causes the brain to shrink and brain cells to eventually die. Alzheimer's disease is the most common cause of dementia — a gradual decline in memory, thinking, behavior and social skills. These changes affect a person's ability to function.

Animal model

To induce AD pathology, a combination of AlCl3 and D-gal will be used to induce pathogenesis of AD detected by beta-amyloid and phosphorylated tau as the disease hallmarks. The combination of these chemicals has been reported to show AD-like behavior. Thus, selecting the chemical concentrations to induce AD and time in the study is appropriate to develop key AD phenotypes.
